MAGNETIC ISLAND

Overview

Lose yourself for a day, a week, or forever on Magnetic Island.‘Maggie' is a charming holiday destination characterised by the fact that it is an island community with a resident population of over 2500. It's true what they say, the attraction truly is Magnetic. Only 8 km from Townsville, Magnetic Island is only a 20 minute ferry ride from the mainland with SeaLink Queensland.

Geography

The contrasting landscape of this Island makes this natural destination truly distinctive. The rocky granite headlands fuse with the 23 bays and beaches (some of which are so isolated that they can only be accessed via foot or boat) studded with magnificent emerald green hoop pine trees, creating a dramatic coastline. Visible from the sea, Mount Cook hovers above the eucalypt woods and green vine forests with sheltered gullies of rainforest. A continental island composed mostly of granite, it was once part of the mainland before the sea level rose about 7500 years ago.

Island History and Heritage

When Captain Cook discovered the island in 1770 he believed that it had a magnetic force causing interference with his compass. Subsequent tests disproved his theory but the name remains. The island's fascinating past is explained in interpretive signs located in Picnic, Cockle, Nelly, Arcadia, Horseshoe Bays and West Point. Visit the Magnetic Island History and Craft Centre in the old Picnic Bay School for more information about the island's heritage and to view a wonderful collection of old photographs and artifacts.

Island Wildlife

With over 2/3 of the Island protected by National Park it is little wonder why there is an abundance of amazing wildlife and walks to discover. For birding enthusiasts Magnetic Island offers over 180 species of birds including colorful characters such as Kookaburras, Red-Tailed Black Cockatoos, Sulphur-Crested Cockatoos, Brahminy Kites and of course the Island's most iconic bird, the Bush Stone-Curlew whose haunting, eerie sounds can be heard at night around the Island.
On land the graceful allied Rock Wallaby can be seen regularly on rocks near the edge of settlements such as the Geoffrey Bay jetty. Here you can hand feed the wallabies and fish that inhabit the local area. Look for koalas in trees around the island, particularly along the Forts walk. They rest motionless amongst eucalypt branches during the day and feed in the late afternoon. Listen for common brushtail possums at night as they feed on flowers, fruit and leaves.

GETTING HERE

Flights to Magnetic Island

Townsville is the airport for Magnetic Island, and connections are made to the island by ferry. All major domestic airlines serve Townsville airport, including Qantas, Jetstar, and Virgin Blue. Gateway cities include Melbourne, Sydney, Canberra, Cairns, Gold Coast, and Brisbane. Several regional carriers also provide service to Townsville, namely Rex, Qantaslink, and Skytrans.
The Fantasea Cruising Magnetic Ferry Terminal is 10 km by road from the airport. All flight arrivals are met by taxi (rough cost $20).
Jetstar Airlines flies direct to Townsville from Melbourne, Sydney, and Brisbane, and has connecting flights from many other cities. Flights start from under $200 from most gateways.
Qantas Airways has direct service from Melbourne (Jetstar), Sydney, and Brisbane as well as connecting flights from other cities.
Virgin Australia offers direct flights to Townsville from Sydney, Brisbane, and Cairns as well as connecting flights from other cities.

Townsville Airport

Townsville, in North Queensland, is the airport for Magnetic Island and connections are made throughout the day to the island by high-speed ferry. The Townsville airport is served daily by all major domestic air carriers as well as several regional airlines. Gateway departure cities include Melbourne, Sydney, Cairns, and Brisbane. The Fantasea Cruising Magnetic Terminal is 10 km by road from the airport, with both taxi and shuttle options (ranging $10-20) available for your connection. Car hire is also available at the Townsville airport, and vehicles may be transported on the ferry with reservations.

Car Hire

Car hire is readily available through the major companies in most cities within Australia. Thus, you may hire a vehicle and drive to Townsville, putting your hire vehicle on our Fantasea Cruising Magnetic ferry for direct service to Nelly Bay. Another option is to travel as a passenger on the ferry, then hire a car upon arrival on Magnetic Island. Car hire is available at affordable rates for the day or for longer periods of time. Vehicles are late model, automatic, and air-conditioned.
Independent Rentals (www.independentrentals.com.au or 1800 678 843) have agreements in place so that hire cars can be taken safely across on the ferry. Thrifty Car Rentals also allow vehicles to travel to Magnetic Island (www.thrifty.com.au or 1300 367 227). Please check your terms and conditions of car hire for more information.
Hiring a car to explore Magnetic Island offers you immense flexibility to enjoy all that the island has to offer. Keep your own schedule, and take in whatever strikes your fancy. Discover a hidden beach cove, snorkel, lounge in the sun, shop, dine, and more.

Freight

Magnetic Island Transport has been operating on Magnetic Island for over 30 years. It is an essential business to the Island, providing a variety of freight from the mainland, Townsville, to Magnetic Island.
Magnetic Island Transport are the main freight carrying service, servicing the businesses and individuals of Magnetic Island. They use a range of trucks of various sizes, including semi trailers to deliver all freight to and from the Island. MIT’s local service includes white Goods;furniture;dry palletized goods;refrigerated/frozen freight;building Supplies;express parcel freight;12m Semi and 6m Tray truck;13.5m Dropdeck lowloader;20′ and 40′ shipping containers;dangerous Goods;over dimensional freight and forklift hire.
